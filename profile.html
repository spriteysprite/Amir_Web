Amir Giles is a performer and choreographer drawing on contemporary dance, contact improvisation and capoeira, several vernacular partner dance forms including tango and swing, as well as  funkstyles such as floating, waving, liquid and popping.  His  experience spans performance, collaborative devising, movement direction and choreography for television, film, theatre, opera, performance art and research projects.
He has worked with Paramount Pictures, Boy George, Film Four, Harbringer Theatre Company, Kate Flatt Productions with the Linbury Theatre Royal Opera House, Watford Palace Theatre and Greenwich Dance Agency.  As well as numerous short choreographies Amir has produced and choreographed two medium length pieces.  'Entangled', a 25 minute collaboration with Tara Pilbrow, fusing contemporary dance, Argentine tango and physical theatre, shown at Resolution, Pulse Festival, Rojo y Negro Tango festival and Transtango at Kings Place Theatre. 'The Illusion' is a 20 minute solo drawing on capoiera, release and funk styles, shown at Transtango, Siobhan Davies Feedback Forum and 'Free to Fall' at Rich Mix.

Performer

As well as performing in many of his own choreographies, Amir has danced for choreographers such as Kate Flatt, Arthur Pita, Kim Brandstrup, Alex Reynolds and Jenni Wrenn for companies including the Royal Opera, English National Opera, Slanjayvah Danza, Sugarfoot Stompers and Livid Productions.

Instructor

Amir has extensive experience with vernacular partner dance forms, choreographing, performing and teaching for numerous schools, events and festivals around the world. Forms include Argentine Tango, Lindy Hop, West Coast Swing, Modern Jive and Nite Club Two Step. Examples include commissions and performances for high profile events at Leicester Square Hippodrome and the Buddah Bar, Dubai, performances for the Princess Royal, collaboration with 'Strictly Come Dancing's Nicole Cutler, and classes and performances at the Berlin Queer Tango Festival, The Royal Ballet School, multiple major Tango schools in London as well as guest teaching in Paris, Berlin, Buenos Aires and Spain. For five years he directed 'Jango', a successful dance organisation that ran events, classes and performances around London and the UK.
